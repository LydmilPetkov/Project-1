Bulgarian Moussaka


Bulgarian Moussaka is a hearty and comforting dish made with potatoes, ground meat, and a yogurt topping. Here's a classical moussaka recipe that fits well with traditional Bulgarian flavors:


Preparations:

  • Prep Time: 30 minutes
  • Cook Time: 1 hour 20 minutes
  • Servings: 4
  • Temperature: 400째F (200째C)
  • Ground beef: 1 lb (450g)
  • Potatoes: 3 lbs (1.36 kg), peeled and cut into small pieces (1/2" or a bit smaller)
  • Tomato paste: 1 tablespoon
  • Cumin: 1 teaspoon
  • Black pepper: 1/2 teaspoon
  • White pepper: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Nutmeg: 1 teaspoon
  • Oil: 2 tablespoons (not needed if using a nonstick pan for frying)
  • Mushroom stock cubes: 1
  • Yogurt: 1 cup (240 ml)
  • Eggs: 2 - 3 (depending on size)
  • Deep baking tin or casserole dish
  • Frying pan
  • Mixing bowls
  • Measuring cups and spoons

Instructions:


  1. In a frying pan, heat the oil and cook the ground beef with tomato paste, cumin, black and white pepper, nutmeg, and salt until the meat crumbles apart.
  2. Drain the excess fat from the meat mixture.
  3. Place half of the potatoes in a deep baking tin and spread the meat mixture over them.
  4. Top the meat with the remaining potatoes.
  5. Add water until only half of the top layer of potatoes is uncovered and break the stock cube over it.
  6. Preheat the oven to 400째F (200째C).
  7. Pour in the yogurt mixture, ensuring it covers the potatoes entirely.
  8. Bake for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the potatoes are done, but be careful not to burn the yogurt lid.
  9. Let the moussaka cool slightly before serving.

Additional tips:


Safety Notes:
Ensure the oven is preheated before placing the moussaka inside. Handle hot dishes with oven mitts.
Substitutions:
Ground beef can be substituted with ground pork or a mix of both. You can also use different types of stock cubes or bouillon.
Storage:
Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat in the oven or microwave.
Pro Tips:
Use a nonstick pan to avoid adding extra oil. Ensure the potatoes are evenly distributed for consistent cooking. Let the moussaka rest for a few minutes before serving to allow the flavors to meld together.
This recipe is not gluten-free or vegan, and it is best served warm. The dish can be made ahead and baked just before serving. A deep baking tin or casserole dish is essential for this recipe.
